The Importance of Vaccinations

A nursing puppy/kitten receives antibodies from its mothers milk that protect it from disease during the first weeks of life. These antibodies gradually decrease during the first few months of a puppys/kittens life. Therefore we give puppies/kittens a series of 3-4 vaccine doses spread out. All adult pets should be vaccinated yearly.

Parvovirus Infection in puppies and Dogs

Parvovirus is a highly contagious virus that causes severe infection in puppies and unvaccinated dogs by invading and destroying white blood cells and the cells of the intestinal lining. Puppies and dogs become infected when they ingest virus that has been shed in the stool of an infected dog.

Heartworm Disease

Heartworm disease is very commom in East Texas. Heartworms are parasites that invade a pet's circulatory system and heart. It is caused by a parasite that lives in the right side of the dog's heart and the nearby large vessels. Highly effective mediation is available to prevent heartworm disease in dogs and cats.

Dental Care

Dental tartar is composed of various mineral salts, organic material, and food particles. In the early stages of accumulation the material is soft, but it quickly hardens into tartar, adheres to teeth and acannot be removed by a toothbruch after 3 days. The best prevention is brushing teeth daily to remove plaque and prevent calculus formation.
